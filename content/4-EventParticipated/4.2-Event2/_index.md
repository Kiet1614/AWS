### Overarching Theme

### Key Highlights



#### 1. Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and FOCI

This topic focuses on improving AWS skills through hands-on learning methods and community programs.

- **AWS Cloud Quest:** learn AWS services through gamification — solve real-world scenarios, practice directly with AWS, and earn rewards upon completing missions
- **Community and training programs:** FOCI and hands-on activities help build a more effective AWS learning path
- **Focus on core services:** instead of learning hundreds of services at once, understand how they work and when to use each one

> **Key message:** The most effective way to learn AWS combines foundational knowledge, hands-on practice, and community participation for continuous exchange and experience sharing.



#### 2. Hackathon

This topic introduces Hackathons as an environment where participants solve a real-world problem within a limited timeframe.

- **Participation workflow:** form ideas → analyze requirements → divide tasks → build a prototype → present the solution
- **Skills developed:** teamwork, communication, time management, problem-solving, and presentation — not just coding
- **Using AI in Hackathons:** support brainstorming, coding, and solution research, but still require understanding the product and explaining technology choices

> **Key message:** The greatest value of a Hackathon is not just winning, but the process of learning, experimenting with ideas, making mistakes, and building a real product in a short time.



#### 3. Why We Always Need Confidence

This topic discusses the role of confidence in learning, work, and career development — especially for students and those new to the technology industry.

- **Confidence is not knowing everything:** it is the ability to ask questions, share opinions, present knowledge, attend interviews, and try new opportunities even when you are not perfect
- **Building confidence through action:** participate in communities, present to others, and proactively exchange ideas
- **You don't need to be skilled enough to start:** confidence is built through experience, accepting mistakes, and continuous improvement

> **Key message:** You don't need to wait until you are skilled enough to begin. Confidence is built through action, experience, accepting mistakes, and continuously improving yourself.



#### 4. The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

This topic uses the iceberg metaphor to describe a software project — users only see the interface and features, while beneath lies a large system of infrastructure, deployment, security, monitoring, logging, backup, CI/CD, and recovery capabilities.

- **The hidden part of a project:** infrastructure, deployment, security, monitoring, logging, backup, CI/CD, and disaster recovery
- **DevOps is more than deployment:** automate build and deployment, manage environments, monitor system health, and detect issues early
- **Local ≠ Production:** a product that runs well on a developer's machine is not necessarily ready for production

> **Key message:** A successful project needs more than working code — it requires stable deployment, monitoring, security, and recovery when incidents occur. DevOps should be considered early, not only after the system encounters problems.



#### 5. The Iceberg of Procrastination

This topic analyzes the root causes behind procrastination — the visible part may be not wanting to study, playing games, or scrolling social media, while beneath lie oversized goals, overwhelm, lack of instant rewards, fear of failure, and difficulty getting started.

- **Root causes:** goals that are too large, overwhelm, lack of immediate rewards, fear of failure, difficulty starting
- **Practical solutions:** break goals into smaller tasks, start with 5–10 minutes, apply the 2-minute rule, build streaks, and create a reward system
- **Gamify learning:** XP, levels, streaks, and feedback help sustain long-term motivation

> **Key message:** To overcome procrastination, don't rely solely on willpower — build an environment and system that makes starting easier. Those who maintain long-term habits are often not the most motivated, but those who build a way of learning that makes them want to return every day.



### Key Takeaways



#### AWS skills and hands-on learning

- Know how to use AWS Cloud Quest to learn through real-world scenarios and gamification
- Understand the benefits of joining communities and programs like FOCI in an AWS learning path
- Focus on deeply learning core services rather than superficially learning hundreds of services



#### Work and product development skills

- Grasp the process and value of Hackathons in solving real-world problems
- Understand the "hidden iceberg" of a project: DevOps, CI/CD, monitoring, security, and disaster recovery
- Recognize that DevOps should be considered early, not only when the system fails



#### Personal development

- Understand that confidence comes from action and experience, not as a prerequisite to begin
- Analyze the root causes of procrastination and how to build a sustainable learning system
- Apply the 2-minute rule, streaks, and gamification to sustain motivation



### Applying to Work

- **Learn AWS:** use Cloud Quest and join the FOCI community to practice AWS services used in your projects
- **Join Hackathons:** develop teamwork, time management, and MVP-building skills in a short timeframe
- **Apply a DevOps mindset:** consider deployment, monitoring, and CI/CD from the early stages of a project
- **Build confidence:** proactively present, ask questions, and share knowledge within your team
- **Overcome procrastination:** break down AWS learning goals, start with 5–10 minutes daily, and maintain streaks
