During my internship at **Amazon Web Services Vietnam** (20 Apr–12 Jul 2026), I participated in the **First Cloud Journey** program and had many meaningful experiences. Below is my honest feedback, hoping it helps FCJ improve for future interns.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**

The FCJ environment is comfortable and approachable. When I first joined, I was quite shy because there was so much I did not know, but my teammates and mentor were always willing to explain again when I asked — even questions I thought were “too basic.” The workspace at Bitexco also helped me focus better than studying alone at home. What I appreciated most is that I never felt judged for being an intern; everyone treated me as a real team member working on the project.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**

My mentor did not just tell me what to do but often asked back, “What do you think you should do?” At first this confused me, but later I understood it was to help me think independently instead of waiting for answers. When I ran into errors deploying DACSWEBSK on AWS, my mentor usually pointed me in a direction rather than doing it for me — I really value that. The admin team supported procedures, documents, and progress reminders clearly, which helped me stay on track with the internship report workload.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**

The work I was assigned aligns well with my IT major, but at a much deeper and more practical level than in school. I learned AWS from the foundations (EC2, IAM, S3) and gradually moved to more complex topics like VPC, RDS, Lambda, ALB, and deploying a real project. I think this is a strength of the program: interns are not just given minor tasks but go through a structured cloud learning path.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunitie**

Beyond AWS technical skills, I learned how to write proposals, workshop documentation, worklogs, and event reflection reports — skills I rarely practiced at university. The three **FCAJ Community Day** sessions were also very valuable: not only about cloud, but also about learning habits, using AI, and career mindset. I improved most in self-learning and teamwork, though communication and presentation are still areas I need to work on.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**

My FCJ peers are friendly and often share experiences with each other. When I struggled with the workshop or did not understand an AWS service, they did not mind explaining again or pointing me to useful documentation. I felt respected even as an intern, which made me ask more questions instead of staying silent out of fear of being wrong.

### Additional Questions

**What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**

I was most satisfied looking back at the journey from “not knowing what AWS is” to being able to deploy and document the **DACSWEBSK** project on the cloud. The feeling of building something real, even if not perfect, made me more confident in the path I chose.

**What do you think should be improved for future interns?**

I think there should be a few more regular check-ins for interns to present progress and receive early feedback, rather than reviewing the entire report only near the end. A clearer roadmap for each phase (learning AWS → project work → writing the report) would also help new interns feel less overwhelmed at the start.

**If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why?**

Yes. I would recommend it to friends who genuinely want to learn cloud and are willing to explore on their own, because the program offers real AWS work and FCJ community participation — not just theory. However, I would also be honest that it requires effort and proactiveness, because there is a lot to learn in a short time.

### Suggestions & Expectations

**Suggestions to improve the internship experience**

- Add mid-term review sessions for worklog, proposal, and workshop progress.
- Share more case studies or lessons from previous interns to give newcomers clearer direction.

**Would you like to stay connected with FCJ/AWS in the future?**

Yes. I want to continue learning AWS in depth, earn certifications, and find opportunities in the cloud field. This internship gave me a clearer direction, though I know I still have much to learn.

**Other comments**

Thank you to FCJ, my mentor, and everyone who created an environment for real hands-on experience. I do not see this as just an internship to earn credits, but as a phase that helped me understand myself better — where I am strong, where I am weak, and what I still need to improve. I hope this feedback is useful, even if it is not complete.
