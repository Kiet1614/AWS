During my internship at **Amazon Web Services Vietnam** from **April 20, 2026** to **July 12, 2026**, I had the opportunity to learn, practice, and apply the knowledge acquired in school to a real-world working environment.

I participated in deploying and documenting the **Event Management (DACSWEBSK)** project on AWS, including drafting the cloud architecture proposal, practicing workshops with services such as EC2, RDS, S3, SES, Lambda, ALB, WAF, and monitoring, while also completing the internship report with worklogs, translated blogs, and reflection reports from three **FCAJ Community Day** sessions. Through this, I improved my skills in **researching and using AWS services**, **analyzing and presenting technical solutions**, **writing documentation and reports**, and **teamwork**.

In terms of work ethic, I always tried to complete assigned tasks, proactively looked things up when I encountered unfamiliar parts, and exchanged ideas with my mentor and teammates. However, I still have limitations in **professional knowledge**, **communication**, and **problem-solving**, so I need to keep improving.

### Needs Improvement

- **Professional knowledge:** need a deeper understanding of how AWS services work together in a complete system, rather than only knowing how to configure individual components
- **Ability to learn:** when encountering new technologies or services, need to absorb and apply knowledge faster instead of taking too long to get familiar
- **Communication:** need to present progress, questions, and opinions more clearly when reporting to my mentor and discussing with the team
- **Problem-solving mindset:** when errors occur during deployment or documentation, need to proactively analyze causes and try solutions before asking for help
- **Progressive mindset:** need to take feedback earlier and adjust my approach during the work process
